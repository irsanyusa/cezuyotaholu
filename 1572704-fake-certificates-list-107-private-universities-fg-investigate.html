Fake Certificates: List of 107 Private Universities FG Wants to Investigate

June 2024 · 6 minute read
  • The Federal Ministry of Education will investigate no fewer than 107 private universities in the country
  • The affected private universities to be probed by the federal government are those established in the last 15 years
  • The move was announced at the launch of a probe of degree racketeering in foreign and local private universities

Legit.ng journalist Adekunle Dada has over 5-year-experience covering basic and tertiary education in Nigeria and worldwide

FCT, Abuja - The Minister of Education, Prof. Tahir Mamman has set up a seven-member inter-ministerial committee to investigate 107 private universities established in the last 15 years.

According to The Nation, Mamman gave the directive after launching a probe of degree racketeering in foreign and local private universities on Tuesday, January 9.

The committee, which includes the Registrar of the Joint Admissions and Matriculation Board (JAMB), Prof. Ishaq Oloyede, the Minister of Youths, Jamila Bio-Ibrahim, and the Minister of Foreign Affairs, Yusuf Tuggar, among others will investigate the allegations within eight weeks.

Fake certificates: Universities in Benin, Togo affected by FG's suspension

Legit.ng also reported that no fewer than 45 universities will be affected by the federal government's decision to suspend evaluation and accreditation for degree certificates from Benin Republic and Togo.

Some of the institutions include Institut Supérieur de Formation Professionnelle (ISFOP), Lakeside University College (LUC) Benin, and African University of Bénin.
